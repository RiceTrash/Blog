01 Day One

Flight and First Explorations

From Zamboanga to Manila, discovering historic Intramuros and the commercial heart of Asia

We kicked off our tour by arriving at Zamboanga Airport at 8:25 AM. After our flight, we landed at NAIA Terminal 3 in Manila around 2 PM and met our tour guide who would accompany us for the next eight days.

Our first destination was Intramuros, Manila's oldest district. Walking through its stone gates transported us back to Spanish colonial Philippines.

The cobblestone streets revealed centuries of history, with Fort Santiago as its centerpiece. Our guide explained the layers of influence:

  • Spanish colonial architecture forming the original structures
  • American military adaptations during their occupation
  • Japanese use of some chambers during World War II

As evening approached, we visited SM Mall of Asia (MOA), one of Asia's largest shopping centers. The waterfront complex spans over 400,000 square meters and serves as an economic hub attracting both international brands and showcasing Filipino entrepreneurship. We ended our first day watching the sunset over Manila Bay from the mall's boardwalk.

Manila American Cemetery

Memorial & Reflection

The Manila American Cemetery offered a solemn contrast to our corporate tours. This 152-acre memorial contains 17,201 graves of U.S. military personnel who died during WWII, arranged in perfect symmetry across pristine lawns.

The central memorial features Pacific campaign maps, while the "Tablets of the Missing" honor 36,285 unrecovered service members. This peaceful tribute to Filipino-American alliance gave us perspective on the freedoms we often take for granted.

People's Park in the Sky

Historical Landmark

People's Park sits 2,150 feet above sea level, offering panoramic views of Taal Volcano and Lake. Originally conceived as "Palace in the Sky" for President Reagan's planned visit (which was canceled), the unfinished structure later became a public park.

Despite occasional rain and fog during our visit, we enjoyed breathtaking glimpses of Taal's unique volcano-within-a-lake formation when the clouds parted. The site's partially completed state creates an interesting contrast with the surrounding natural beauty.

üé¢

Sky Ranch

Adventure Park

Sky Ranch Tagaytay offered a welcome shift in pace from our corporate tours and historical visits. The amusement park, perched on the ridge overlooking Taal Lake, combines thrill rides with spectacular natural scenery. While some of our group enjoyed the tamer attractions and photo opportunities, others challenged themselves with the park's signature rides.

The Viking ship ride quickly became the highlight of our visit, swinging us to dizzying heights with Taal Lake providing a dramatic backdrop. The combination of adrenaline, collective screaming, and breathtaking views created a bonding experience unlike any other on our tour. Between rides, we sampled local street foods including taho (silken tofu with sago pearls and caramel syrup), fish balls with spicy vinegar sauce, and the region's famous beef tapa. These shared experiences‚Äîalternating between thrill-seeking and culinary exploration‚Äîstrengthened our connections as classmates and future colleagues.

Strawberry Farm

Local Experience

La Trinidad Strawberry Farm spans over 80 hectares in the valley floor just outside Baguio City proper. Despite our early arrival at 6 AM, the farm was already active with farmers tending to the rows of strawberries that thrive in the cool mountain climate. Though it wasn't peak harvest season, we still found sections with ripe berries and enjoyed the farm's signature treat‚Äîstrawberry taho, where the traditional sweet tofu snack is enhanced with fresh strawberry syrup made on-site.

Beyond strawberries, the farm has diversified into other high-value crops including lettuce, broccoli, and cut flowers. Local vendors lined the entrance selling strawberry-themed products from preserves and vinegars to strawberry-shaped souvenir items. Our purchases of strawberry jam, dried berries, and strawberry wine would later serve as pasalubong (homecoming gifts) for family members, sharing a taste of Baguio's cool climate specialties with loved ones back home in Mindanao.

Chinese Bell Church

Cultural Heritage

The Chinese Bell Church showcases Baguio's multicultural heritage through a tranquil complex blending Taoist, Buddhist, and Confucian traditions. The temple reflects significant Chinese influence in Philippine culture.

We explored manicured gardens with colorful pagoda-style structures and admired the detailed craftsmanship of the buildings and ornate gate. The central worship hall features traditional architecture with vibrant red accents symbolizing good fortune‚Äîa peaceful contrast to our otherwise busy itinerary.

The Mansion

Presidential Residence

The Mansion serves as the official summer residence of the President of the Philippines, built in 1908 during American colonial rule to escape Manila's sweltering heat. While the main building isn't open to the public, we were able to admire the structure's colonial architecture from outside and take photos at the ornate main gate‚Äîan elaborate ironwork masterpiece patterned after the gates of Buckingham Palace.

The presidential seal prominently displayed at the entrance reminded us of the residence's official status. The well-maintained gardens surrounding the mansion feature manicured lawns and vibrant flower beds typical of Baguio's cool climate horticulture. Our guide explained that while presidents now visit less frequently than in the past, the mansion remains an important symbol of Philippine governance and a reminder of the city's status as the country's summer capital.

Mines View Park

Cultural Hub

Mines View Park provided both stunning natural vistas and a vibrant cultural marketplace. From the observation deck, we could see the panoramic view of Benguet's old copper and gold mines and the surrounding mountains of the Cordillera range‚Äîa landscape that tells the story of the region's mining history.

The park area has evolved into a lively market where local vendors sell everything from ube jam and strawberry products to handwoven textiles and wooden crafts. Many of us enjoyed the unique experience of renting traditional Igorot tribal outfits for photos, complete with woven vests and headdresses. The cool mountain air was filled with the scents of local street food, including freshly baked Baguio buns and hot taho with strawberry syrup. We sampled various local delicacies while browsing the souvenir stalls, making this stop both a cultural and culinary highlight of our Baguio visit.

Burnham Park & SM Baguio

Urban Nature

Our final full day in Baguio was split between the historic Burnham Park and the modern comfort of SM Baguio mall. Burnham Park, designed by American architect Daniel Burnham in 1904, forms the green heart of the city. We strolled along the man-made lake where some of our group tried paddleboating, while others explored the rose garden and orchidarium filled with highland flowers and plants.

The park's wide, pine-lined pathways provided a perfect setting for reflection on our journey so far. In the afternoon, we headed to SM Baguio, whose unique terraced architecture adapts to the mountainside location. Beyond shopping, we enjoyed the panoramic views from the mall's view deck, where the entire city lay below us. This final day balanced Baguio's natural beauty with urban convenience‚Äîa fitting conclusion to our highland adventure before returning to Manila.
